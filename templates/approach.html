Our Approach

A systematic, rules-based investment process combining factor-based stock selection, regime-aware allocation, and rigorous out-of-sample validation.

Investment Philosophy

We believe that markets reward certain persistent risk premia — momentum, value, quality, and low volatility — that can be harvested systematically. Rather than relying on human judgement or conviction-based stock picking, we use a quantitative model that ranks stocks on 13 measurable factors and constructs portfolios purely based on data.

Equally important is knowing when to invest. Our 7-signal regime detection ensemble continuously monitors market conditions and adjusts the portfolio's allocation between equities, gold, and debt. This reduces drawdowns without sacrificing long-term returns.

Every aspect of our methodology is walk-forward validated — we never use future data to make past decisions. This discipline ensures that our track record reflects what an investor would have actually experienced, not an optimised backtest.

13-Factor Stock Selection Model

Stocks from the NIFTY LargeMidcap 250 universe are ranked on each factor. Top 20 stocks by composite z-score are selected, conviction-weighted, and rebalanced monthly.

Momentum (7 factors)

  • 12-month price momentum Trailing return excl. last month
  • 6-month price momentum Medium-term trend strength
  • 3-month price momentum Short-term acceleration
  • 52-week high proximity Distance from annual peak
  • Volume-weighted momentum Turnover-adjusted returns
  • Risk-adjusted momentum Return per unit volatility
  • Earnings momentum Quarter-on-quarter EPS change

Quality (4 factors)

  • Return on Equity (ROE) Profitability efficiency
  • Return on Capital (ROCE) Capital allocation quality
  • Operating Profit Margin Business economics strength
  • Debt-to-Equity ratio Balance sheet conservatism

Value (1 factor)

  • Earnings Yield (EPS/Price) Valuation attractiveness

Low Volatility (1 factor)

  • Realised volatility (60-day) Risk-reduction tilt

Regime-Aware Allocation

A 7-signal ensemble generates a continuous confidence score from -1 (risk-off) to +1 (risk-on). The allocation between equities, gold, and debt shifts dynamically based on this score.

Signal Description Timeframe
Trend (SMA) NIFTY 50 price vs 200-day moving average Long-term
Trend (EMA) NIFTY 50 exponential moving average crossover Medium-term
Momentum Rate of change in NIFTY 50 over 60 days Medium-term
Volatility India VIX relative to historical range Short-term
Breadth Percentage of NIFTY 500 stocks above 200-day MA Broad market
Credit Spread Corporate vs government bond yield differential Macro
FII Flow Foreign institutional investor net buy/sell trend Sentiment

Allocation Mapping

Confidence +0.7 to +1.0: 100% equity (strong bull regime)
Confidence +0.3 to +0.7: 80-100% equity, 0-10% gold, 0-10% debt
Confidence -0.3 to +0.3: 40-80% equity, 10-30% gold, 10-30% debt
Confidence -0.7 to -0.3: 10-40% equity, 30-45% gold, 30-45% debt
Confidence -1.0 to -0.7: 0-10% equity, 45% gold, 45% debt (defensive)

During the COVID-19 crash (March 2020), the regime score dropped to -0.85 and the portfolio shifted to 3% equity, limiting the drawdown to under 12% while NIFTY fell 38%.

Walk-Forward Validation

The strongest test of any investment strategy is whether it works on data it has never seen. We use walk-forward validation — the gold standard in systematic trading research.

Training period (2 years) Out-of-sample test (6 months)

How it works: In each window, the model optimises factor weights on the training data (2 years), then applies those weights to the subsequent test period (6 months) that it has never seen. The portfolio track record is built entirely from these test periods — none of the reported returns come from in-sample data.

Survivorship bias elimination: At each point in time, the stock universe is reconstructed from official NSE constituent PDFs. We maintain 96 point-in-time snapshots covering 338 unique stocks, ensuring that only stocks that were actually in the index at that time are considered. This prevents the common bias of including stocks that survived to the present while excluding those that were delisted or removed.
