Regime Confidence Over Time

How allocation works: A 7-signal regime model (price vs moving averages, momentum, breadth) produces a continuous confidence score from -1 (strong bear) to +1 (strong bull). In strong bull regimes, the portfolio is ~90-100% equity. In strong bear regimes, it shifts to gold and debt, going as low as 3% equity during the COVID crash (Mar 2020). The allocation is recalculated at each monthly rebalance.

Performance Comparison

Walk-Forward Portfolio: Selects top 20 stocks from ~250 eligible using 13 factors (momentum, value, quality, low volatility). Factor weights optimized on rolling 2-year training windows, tested on subsequent 6-month out-of-sample periods. Regime-aware allocation shifts to gold/debt in bear markets. Staggered daily rebalance (21 tranches) with 15% max single-stock weight cap. 10bps round-trip slippage applied.
NIFTY 50 (Benchmark): Passive 100% investment in India's flagship large-cap index. No rebalancing, no allocation changes. This is the baseline that any active strategy must beat.
Static 80/15/5: Fixed allocation of 80% NIFTY 50, 15% gold (GOLDBEES), 5% debt (6.5% annual). Rebalanced to target weights. Represents a simple diversified allocation without any active signals.
Gold (GOLDBEES): 100% investment in gold ETF. Included to show the hedging asset that the portfolio shifts into during bear regimes.
50/50 Gold + Stock Pick: Same 20-stock selection as Walk-Forward but with a fixed 50% equity, 50% gold allocation rebalanced monthly. No regime model — tests whether simple gold hedging captures most of the regime model's benefit.
Classic 60/40: Traditional balanced portfolio with 60% equity (NIFTY 50) and 40% debt. The conservative benchmark for risk-adjusted comparison.
All returns are hypothetical and for research purposes only. Walk-forward testing with point-in-time constituent data (no survivorship bias). Past performance does not guarantee future results. Slippage of 10bps per round-trip applied. Does not include management fees, taxes, or impact costs beyond slippage.
